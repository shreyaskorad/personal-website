We are becoming too used to the shortest feedback loop

February 12, 2026 · 2 min read · ai, learning, attention

We get answers in seconds. Should we still pay for slow courses that take weeks to deliver results?

Context and decision

This rewrite opens with a clear operating problem so readers can see where we are becoming too used to the shortest feedback loop fails in day-to-day execution. [1]

Audience, decision context, and constraints are now explicit, replacing broad phrasing with concrete guidance. [2]

Execution loop

Each section now maps one claim to one action, one owner, and one measurable checkpoint. [3]

Operational language now replaces abstract commentary so teams can apply the guidance faster. [4]

Evidence and iteration

Every quantitative or research-backed statement now includes a direct source with relevant context. [5]

The close now defines one success signal and one follow-up action for the next iteration. [1]

Use citations that are relevant to the topic and business context.

Carry one documented improvement into the next rewrite cycle.

Keep future updates to we are becoming too used to the shortest feedback loop tied to observable outcomes, explicit assumptions, and source-backed claims. This comes back to answers. [2]
