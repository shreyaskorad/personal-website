If everyone is losing jobs, who pays for AI infrastructure?

2026-02-07 · 2 min read · AI, Economy, Infrastructure

If everyone is losing jobs, who pays for AI infrastructure? It is funded by concentrated capital and by customers, and the risk is who gets left out.

AI infrastructure is expensive. Data centers, GPUs, and electricity. That money is concentrated in a few companies and investors.

The subscription model creates a real circular dependency. People need jobs to pay for AI subscriptions, but those subscriptions cut jobs that would pay for them.

The risk is that AI becomes a moat for the already advantaged. Open-source, public investment, regulation, and access programs can help spread the benefits beyond the big players.

It is not just who pays for AI, but who gets to use it. The focus must be on ensuring broad access rather than maximizing profits. This is a systemic issue that needs deliberate solutions from governments, companies, and society at large together.
