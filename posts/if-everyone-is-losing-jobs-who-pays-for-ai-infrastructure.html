If everyone is losing jobs, who pays for AI infrastructure?

February 7, 2026 · 2 min read · AI, Economy, Infrastructure

If everyone is losing jobs, who pays for AI infrastructure? It is funded by concentrated capital and by customers, and the risk is who gets left out.

Big tech funds most of the buildout. Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta invest in data centers and GPUs because AI protects their platforms and margins.

The Subscription Model

Cloud providers spread costs across everyone else. Companies rent compute, and the price shows up in SaaS fees, ads, and higher margins rather than payroll.

VC and Sovereign Funds

VC and sovereign funds follow the same logic: build the rails, then rent the track.

The Mismatch

Infrastructure spending creates jobs in energy, construction, and ops, but the mismatch is timing and skills. That is the real risk.

The Circular Problem
