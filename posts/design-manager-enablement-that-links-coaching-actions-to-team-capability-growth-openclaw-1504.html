Design manager enablement that links coaching actions to team capability growth

February 19, 2026 · 2 min read · learning, ai

Design manager enablement that links coaching actions to team capability growth matters only when it changes a real decision at work, not when it adds another layer of learning activity.

Design manager enablement that links coaching actions to team capability growth matters only when it changes a real decision at work, not when it adds another layer of learning activity.

I see the same pattern in weekly check-ins: people discuss progress, but they skip the decision logic that created rework.

Start with one intervention and one checkpoint, then compare before-after behavior in the next cycle instead of launching a broad initiative.

This keeps learning close to execution because claims are tested against daily work outcomes, not abstract content goals.

Which recurring decision in your workflow is the right place to test this next?
