Ai feedback works only when it supports human coaching

February 11, 2026 · 2 min read · ai, learning, lxd

I like the speed of AI feedback, but I have seen teams mistake speed for learning. Fast comments help only when they connect to a coach, a context, and a real performance goal in daily work.

What if learning improved this week’s decisions instead of next quarter’s compliance scores? A 2024 Deloitte study found that 15% boost in overall performance, highlighting the shift from static courses to embedded, real‑time guidance. By weaving AI‑driven prompts directly into tools, teams receive instant feedback that sharpens judgment and reduces error rates. Measuring success moves beyond completion percentages to metrics like time‑to‑competence and error reduction, which correlate with a 15% boost in overall performance according to a 2024 Deloitte analysis. Leaders can design lightweight learning cues that surface at the point of decision, ensuring knowledge is applied when it matters most. This approach not only accelerates skill acquisition but also builds a culture of continuous improvement, where every task becomes a chance to grow. If capability is the goal, treat decision quality as an operating metric, not a training byproduct.
