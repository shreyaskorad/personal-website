AI feedback works only when it supports human coaching

February 11, 2026 · 2 min read · ai, learning, lxd

I like the speed of AI feedback, but I have seen teams mistake speed for learning. Fast comments help only when they connect to a coach, a context, and a real performance goal in daily work.

We use AI for immediate feedback on practice tasks, then managers review patterns in weekly conversations. That keeps reflection grounded in real work, helps people understand why a better choice matters in context, and prevents the tool from becoming another noisy notification stream.

Does this feedback improve judgment when pressure is real? If the answer is unclear, we simplify the prompt, improve the coaching conversation, and measure behavior in the next cycle. The tool is useful, but the capability shift still depends on people taking better actions in tough moments.

AI feedback becomes powerful when it strengthens human coaching and decision quality in the workplace.
