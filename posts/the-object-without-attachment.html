The Object Without Attachment

2026-02-08 · 2 min read · ai, emotion, work

Humans are wired to bond with things we work through, not just finish. When AI compresses effort, you get the result without the emotional imprint that normally makes it feel yours.

We bond with the work we do, not just the finished product. The process of building, fixing, and refining creates attachment. That attachment makes the output feel like ours. It makes it meaningful.

AI compresses effort. It removes the struggle. The result appears, but the emotional imprint remains outside. You see the object, but the attachment isn't there. You're holding something that feels like yours, but it doesn't feel built by you.

This isn't a critique. It's an observation. When effort disappears, attachment disappears with it. The question isn't whether AI is good or bad. The question is what we lose when the bridge between thought and output becomes frictionless.

The object is there… but the attachment isn't. You see the result without the emotional imprint that makes it feel yours.
