Where We Go From Here

2026-02-08 · 2 min read · productivity, workflow, ai

My work now starts in an IDE or an AI chat interface, where I can move from thought to usable output with much less friction.

My work now starts in an IDE or an AI chat interface because these tools let me think in drafts, test ideas quickly, and ship without spending energy on presentation polish too early.

The biggest shift has been moving from formatting to problem solving. Instead of polishing slides first, I define the argument, pressure-test assumptions, and shape the output around the decision I want to support.

That does not mean old tools are useless. It means tool choice should follow intent. If I need deep iteration and clear reasoning, code and conversational interfaces give me a better working surface.

Where are your current tools slowing down your thinking? Start there, redesign that part of your workflow, and let the rest of your stack evolve around better judgment and faster execution.
