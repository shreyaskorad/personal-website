I see the same pattern in weekly check-ins: people discuss progress, but they skip the decision logic that created rework.

Start with one intervention and one checkpoint, then compare before-after behavior in the next cycle instead of launching a broad initiative.

That approach protects quality because teams refine what works in practice and drop what only sounds good on paper.
