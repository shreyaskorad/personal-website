Behaviour science: evidence triage ritual to improve intervention accuracy for weekly business

February 21, 2026 · 2 min read · behaviour-science, decision-quality, learning-design

In behaviour science, teams improve results when a evidence triage ritual ties learning choices to intervention adoption ratio each week.

Teams stall when no single owner tracks intervention adoption ratio after each learning intervention in live work. [1]

Define one baseline, one target, and one review meeting before starting any new intervention. [2]

In this section, state one risk and one mitigation so teams can act without ambiguity. [1]

First, choose one intervention and assign one manager owner for the next seven days. [2]

Next, log one observation from real work and compare it with the baseline metric. [1]

In this section, link one decision point to one measurable outcome and one owner. [2]

Then review results in a weekly cadence and decide whether to keep, change, or stop. [1]

If impact is flat, audit signal quality, intervention design, and manager follow-through before adding complexity. [2]

Weekly, review decision quality and behavior transfer, measure one intervention, and increase only what improves execution for your team. [1]
