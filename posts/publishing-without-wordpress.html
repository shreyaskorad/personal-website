Publishing Without the WordPress Headache

February 5, 2026 · 2 min read · Productivity, Writing, Web

I keep coming back to a simple question: can I get my ideas onto my website without the WordPress circus? Plugins, logins, image hunts, and endless formatting?

A lighter way to publish

Yes, it’s possible. A lightweight site on GitHub Pages means the design is already set. All I need is the core idea. OpenClaw can turn that idea into a clean post that matches the look and feel of my site, pull a fitting Unsplash image, and ship it as a new page. No dashboard, no plugin conflicts, no surprise bills.

Time saved is real money saved

When I look at the old workflow, most of it was repetitive: finding a theme, resizing images, fixing margins, pasting drafts, re-checking previews. That’s time and labor cost that doesn’t make the writing any better. Automating those steps doesn’t replace the thinking, it protects it. The energy I used to spend on busywork now goes into shaping the message.

If the boring parts are handled, the real work is thinking.
