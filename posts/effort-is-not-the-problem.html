Effort is not the problem. Your learning system is

April 16, 2026 · 2 min read · learning, modern-ld, behavior-science

If you are putting in the hours but the learning still does not stick, the bottleneck is rarely effort. It is the system.

In Professor Game episode 429, study coach Tom Vorselen described failing courses despite high effort because his learning methods were weak.

This is the pattern I see in workplace learning too. We measure time spent and call it effort, but the method quietly decides the outcome.

Rereading, highlighting, and cramming can create familiarity, but they often fail at the one thing you need at work: recall under pressure.

Two evidence-backed moves change the slope. First, retrieval practice: force recall with short questions, prompts, or teaching, instead of re-consuming the material.

Second, spacing: bring the same idea back across days and weeks so memory is rebuilt, not temporarily inflated.

A modern L&D system bakes this in. Ship a weekly learning loop: one 5 minute retrieval prompt, one spaced follow-up, and one piece of feedback that shows progress and gaps.

If you want, I can help you retrofit your learning program into a retrieval and spacing system in one workshop. Start this week by picking one skill, writing five retrieval prompts, and scheduling two spaced check-ins.
