Use data-informed learning decisions, not assumptions

February 11, 2026 · 2 min read · data-informed learning, LxD, learning analytics, practical metrics, decision quality

Learning teams often make decisions based on gut feeling instead of evidence. I recently watched a LxD team redesign their onboarding program after they noticed users completing modules at different rates. The new version improved completion by 15 percent without any additional features or costs.

Ask why learners struggle, what information they skip, and which programs drive business outcomes. One healthcare training program redesigned their content based on drop-off points in learner analytics. Within six months their new program had 22 percent higher retention.

Combine completion rates with performance metrics and direct feedback from learners and managers. When a retail company examined both data sources, they discovered their classroom training wasn't translating to store performance, leading them to redesign the approach entirely.

Teams that test and measure consistently see faster improvements than those that wait for perfect data.

Make one data-driven change each month instead of big structural overhauls
