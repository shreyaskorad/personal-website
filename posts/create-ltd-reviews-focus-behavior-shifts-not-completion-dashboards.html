Create L&D reviews that focus on behavior shifts not completion dashboards

Wed 2026-02-18 · 2 min read · ld, data, learning-transfer

L&D reviews often default to tracking completion rates and quiz scores. This creates a surface-level metric that tells you nothing about whether learning actually changed behavior.

You can have perfect completion dashboards while teams repeat the same mistakes week after week. Review your current L&D evaluation process.

Ask yourself what one team member has genuinely changed in how they work as a result of your last training program. If you cannot answer this clearly, your reviews are measuring the wrong thing.

A practical approach is to add behavior-specific questions to your review framework. Instead of asking "Did they finish the module?

", ask "What different decision would they make this week because of this learning? " This shifts the focus from passive consumption to active application.

Choose one team ritual this week to test create l&d reviews that focus on behavior shifts not completion dashboards, and review outcomes in your next cycle.
