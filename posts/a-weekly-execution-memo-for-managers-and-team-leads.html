A weekly execution memo for managers and team leads

March 6, 2026 · 2 min read · learning-design, decision-quality, field-note

Last week in the quarterly capability review, the blocker was not motivation. It was missing decision structure. Teams were busy, but coaching and execution feedback were disconnected.

The pressure point showed up in the quarterly capability review: teams had activity but weak decision follow-through.

The first fix was narrow: anchor coaching to improve measurable behavior change and review it weekly against handoff rework rate with a 15-20% improvement target.

Test one move inspired by optimizing Sales Excellence Steel Product Markets through in a single team workflow and review the behavior shift next week.

The team used a simple loop: Signal -> Decision -> Action -> Review.

After the next weekly review, the frontline manager could name one better decision and one avoided rework loop.

Tradeoff: forcing one metric improves focus but can hide adjacent risks; review one side effect each week.

Keep 1 evidence check: compare baseline and week-two behavior data before scaling.

Remove any ritual that fails to change an observed decision in the next review.

Start with 1 team, 1 metric, and 1 review rhythm. Owner: frontline manager. First step: choose one decision in the quarterly capability review. Review on the next weekly review. Try this once with your team this week and compare what changes before the next decision cycle.
