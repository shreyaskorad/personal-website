A weekly execution memo for managers and team leads

March 6, 2026 · 2 min read · learning-design, decision-quality, field-note

Last week in the pre-launch risk review, the blocker was not motivation. It was missing decision structure. Teams were busy, but coaching and execution feedback were disconnected.

The pressure point showed up in the pre-launch risk review: teams had activity but weak decision follow-through.

The first fix was narrow: anchor coaching to improve measurable behavior change and review it weekly against repeat escalation frequency with a 15-20% improvement target.

Test one move inspired by optimizing Sales Excellence Steel Product Markets through in a single team workflow and review the behavior shift next week.

The team used a simple loop: Decision checkpoint -> Coaching nudge -> Workflow action -> Evidence review.

Within two cycles, the conversation moved from generic updates to specific trade-offs and ownership.

Keep 1 evidence check: capture one friction point per week and test whether the intervention removes it.

Drop any step that does not improve decision quality after two review cycles.
