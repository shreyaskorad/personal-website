The backstory

From advertising to learning design

I graduated in media with a specialisation in advertising and spent a few years in the ad world. Somewhere along the way, I got pulled into gamification — the idea that game mechanics could shape behaviour and drive real engagement fascinated me.

That interest became a bridge. I realised that the same principles that make games compelling could make learning experiences genuinely effective. In 2019, I joined Upside Learning as a consultant, and I've been there since — moving through learning experience design, solutions, pre-sales, and now working across consulting and innovation initiatives.

Today, I focus on how gamification, learning science, business-problem consulting, data, and AI come together to shape new-age learning strategies and experiences.

What I believe

Learning is not about the curriculum

If I had to distil it down to one thing — most learning programmes get it wrong because they focus on delivering a curriculum. The real question is different: what tasks do people need to do better at work, and how can we help them do that?

To deliver on that promise, you need to pull from many places — engaging game mechanics, design principles, storytelling, learning science, and data. No single framework does it all. The craft is in knowing which levers to pull and when.

Solve for the task, not the syllabus

Learning should close the gap between how people work today and how they need to work tomorrow.

Design for behaviour, not just knowledge

If people don't change what they do, the training didn't work — no matter how good the content was.

Let data tell the story

Engagement scores and completion rates aren't impact. Tie learning back to the business problem it was built to solve.
