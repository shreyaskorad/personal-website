## Prioritization Rules

### Journaling Priority Rules

**Rule 1: Quality Over Quantity**
- A thoughtful 10-minute entry is more valuable than a rushed 30-minute entry
- Focus on depth of reflection rather than word count
- Consistency beats intensity

**Rule 2: Morning Ritual Priority**
- Journal before any other activities in the morning
- Creates foundation for the day
- Sets intentional tone before distractions arise

**Rule 3: Evening Reflection Priority**
- Journal before bed to clear the mind
- Process the day and prepare for rest
- Capture final thoughts and release

**Rule 4: Non-Negotiable Minimum**
- Minimum 3-4 journal entries per week for sustainable habit
- Missing 2-3 days doesn't break the habit
- Consistency is more important than daily perfection

**Rule 5: Simple Entry Priority**
- Start with the basic template structure
- Don't overcomplicate with fancy formats
- Simplicity supports consistency

**Rule 6: Freedom from Judgment**
- Write without worrying about grammar or structure
- What gets written stays in the journal
- Privacy enables honesty and vulnerability

## Review Cadence

### Daily Review (End of Each Day)

**Time**: 10 minutes before bed
**Purpose**: Process the day, clear the mind, prepare for rest

**Review Questions**:
1. Did I complete today's journal entry?
2. Did I follow the basic structure and intention-setting?
3. What were my biggest wins today?
4. What challenges did I face and how did I handle them?
5. What did I learn about myself today?
6. What am I grateful for today?
7. What do I want to carry forward tomorrow?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete the daily journal entry using the template
- Set tomorrow's intention
- Record final thoughts before sleep
- Ensure the mind is clear and ready for rest

### Weekly Review (End of Each Week)

**Time**: Sunday Evening, 30 minutes
**Purpose**: Analyze patterns, identify growth, plan improvements

**Review Questions**:
1. Did I maintain consistency with journaling this week?
2. What themes emerged in my writing?
3. What patterns did I notice about my thoughts and behaviors?
4. What insights did I discover about myself?
5. What challenges repeated or persisted?
6. What can I improve next week?
7. What am I curious about for next week?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete the weekly journal review template
- Identify patterns and insights
- Record growth observations
- Set intentions for next week
- Adjust habits if needed

### Monthly Review (End of Each Month)

**Time**: Last Sunday Evening, 45 minutes
**Purpose**: Assess long-term growth, refine practice, strengthen habit

**Review Questions**:
1. Did I make meaningful progress on my personal growth goals?
2. What themes dominated my writing this month?
3. What patterns are emerging in my personal development?
4. What insights have been the most valuable?
5. What challenges have I consistently faced?
6. What has worked well about my journaling practice?
7. What needs to change for next month?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete the monthly journal analysis template
- Identify long-term patterns and trends
- Assess growth and development
- Refine the journaling practice for continued improvement
- Set next month's priorities
