## Prioritization Rules

### Pocket Notebook Priority Framework

**Rule 1: Capture > Processing**
Prioritize capturing thoughts immediately. Processing can happen later during structured journaling sessions. Quick captures should always happen; processing decisions determine when to expand entries.

**Rule 2: Method A and B > Method C**
Always use Methods A and B first. Method C (expanded entry) should only be used when a quick capture requires immediate expansion or has significant value. Never skip capture methods.

**Rule 3: Process at Morning or Evening**
Expand Method A/B entries either in the morning (processing overnight captures) or evening (processing daily captures). Never process entries multiple times throughout the day.

**Rule 4: One Entry per Method per Session**
Limit to 5-10 quick captures per session. Process 2-3 entries maximum per session. Quality over quantity prevents burnout and maintains consistency.

**Rule 5: Notebook Rotation Priority**
New notebook when: [ ] Previous notebook is full  [ ] 6+ months have passed  [ ] Started new chapter

**Rule 6: Consistency Over Volume**
A complete Method A capture is better than an incomplete Method C entry. Consistent small efforts beat infrequent large efforts.

**Rule 7: Habit > Content Quality**
Focus on habit formation first. Content quality improves naturally as you practice. Don't let perfectionism block your practice.

## Review Cadence

### Daily Review (End of Each Day)

**Time**: 10 minutes before bed
**Purpose**: Process daily captures, expand Method A/B entries, set intention for tomorrow

**Review Questions**:
1. Did I complete daily captures using Methods A or B?
2. Did I process any entries to Method C?
3. What were my top 3 captures today?
4. What do I want to capture tomorrow?
5. Did I use the notebook regularly?

**Review Actions**:
- Process 2-3 Method A/B entries to Method C
- Expand important captures from overnight
- Set tomorrow's morning intention
- Ensure notebook is available for tomorrow

### Weekly Review (End of Each Week)

**Time**: Sunday Evening, 20 minutes
**Purpose**: Review weekly progress, analyze patterns, plan improvements

**Review Questions**:
1. Did I maintain consistent capture habits?
2. What patterns did I notice in my thinking?
3. What ideas were most useful this week?
4. What processing methods worked best?
5. How should I improve next week?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete weekly review template
- Analyze capture patterns and usefulness
- Identify what's working and what needs adjustment
- Set next week's priorities and goals
- Decide if new notebook is needed

### Monthly Review (End of Each Month)

**Time**: Last Sunday Evening, 30 minutes
**Purpose**: Comprehensive monthly assessment, evaluate habit strength

**Review Questions**:
1. Have I maintained consistent notebook practice?
2. What patterns have emerged over the month?
3. Which capture methods are most effective?
4. How useful are my captured ideas?
5. What improvements should I make?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete monthly habit tracker
- Analyze overall habit strength
- Identify monthly best practices
- Plan monthly improvements
- Decide if notebook rotation needs adjustment
