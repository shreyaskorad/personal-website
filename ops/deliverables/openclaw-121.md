## Prioritization Rules

### Dashboard Improvement Priority Framework

**Rule 1: Impact-Based Selection**
Prioritize improvements based on impact on user outcomes rather than technical complexity or implementation effort. High-impact improvements that significantly improve user experience, decision-making, or operational efficiency take precedence.

**Rule 2: User Feedback Integration**
Direct user feedback is prioritized over internal assumptions. Issues and requests from actual users receive higher priority than ideas generated internally.

**Rule 3: Critical vs. Nice-to-Have**
Distinguish between critical issues that negatively impact users or operations vs. nice-to-have enhancements. Critical issues are addressed immediately; nice-to-haves are scheduled for future implementation.

**Rule 4: Feasibility Balance**
Consider implementation feasibility and resources needed. Highly impactful but impossible to implement within reasonable constraints should be documented but not pursued immediately.

**Rule 5: Quick Wins First**
Identify and implement quick wins—improvements with high impact and low effort. These provide immediate value and build momentum for larger projects.

**Rule 6: Long-term Value**
Balance immediate improvements with investments in longer-term enhancements that provide sustained value over time.

## Review Cadence

### Alternate-Day Review Schedule

**Schedule Structure**:
- **Day 1 Review**: Focus on user feedback, critical issues, and immediate improvements
- **Day 2 Review**: Focus on dashboard performance, data accuracy, and long-term improvements
- **Repeat**: Alternate days for continuous review and improvement

**Review Day 1 (Day-Based Review)**:
- **Time**: 15-20 minutes before or after daily review
- **Focus**: User feedback, critical issues, immediate improvements
- **Actions**: 
  - Review recent user feedback and issues
  - Identify critical improvements needed
  - Prioritize quick wins for immediate implementation
  - Update improvement tracker

**Review Day 2 (Data-Based Review)**:
- **Time**: 15-20 minutes before or after daily review
- **Focus**: Dashboard performance, data accuracy, long-term improvements
- **Actions**:
  - Review performance metrics and health scores
  - Analyze user engagement and satisfaction data
  - Identify data quality issues
  - Plan longer-term improvements

**Weekly Review (Always on Day 1)**:
- **Time**: 30 minutes before or after daily review on Day 1
- **Focus**: Weekly progress, priorities, planning
- **Actions**:
  - Review week's improvements and completion rates
  - Update monthly tracking documents
  - Plan next week's priorities
  - Assess dashboard health score

**Monthly Review (Last day of month)**:
- **Time**: 45 minutes before or after daily review
- **Focus**: Comprehensive assessment and planning
- **Actions**:
  - Complete monthly health scorecard
  - Review improvement completion rates
  - Analyze trends and patterns
  - Update long-term plans
