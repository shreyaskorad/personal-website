# Improve judgment without adding course fatigue

I once worked with a leadership team that kept sending people to more training sessions to fix poor judgment. The training never stuck because they never practiced making decisions in the real context. I suggested they let the team make real decisions and learn from the outcomes instead of safe simulations. Their production errors decreased while their confidence in handling complex situations increased. They built better judgment through actual work experience.

## Core point

Build judgment through practice in real work contexts instead of training courses. A leadership team stopped sending people to training and let them make real decisions. They learned from outcomes instead of safe simulations. Their production errors decreased while their confidence increased. Teams that learn through actual work situations develop stronger judgment than those who practice in artificial scenarios.

## Practical takeaway

What single decision area could your team practice in real work contexts without causing damage? Design one structured learning opportunity around a low-risk decision that still requires judgment. Track outcomes and discuss them openly afterward.

Judgment improves when people practice in situations that matter. Teams that embed learning into real work create environments where decisions get better over time. Safe training often feels disconnected from actual challenges. Your team needs autonomy, not more courses.
