## Objective

## Deliverable Body

**Weekly Fasting Schedule**:
The following 7-day intermittent fasting schedule provides structure while allowing flexibility based on daily energy levels and commitments:

**Monday**: 16:8 fast (8-hour eating window: 12:00 PM - 8:00 PM)
**Tuesday**: 16:8 fast (8-hour eating window: 1:00 PM - 9:00 PM)
**Wednesday**: 18:6 fast (6-hour eating window: 12:00 PM - 6:00 PM)
**Thursday**: 16:8 fast (8-hour eating window: 1:00 PM - 9:00 PM)
**Friday**: 20:4 fast (4-hour eating window: 2:00 PM - 6:00 PM)
**Saturday**: 16:8 fast (8-hour eating window: 1:00 PM - 9:00 PM)
**Sunday**: 16:8 fast (8-hour eating window: 12:00 PM - 8:00 PM)

This progressive approach gradually increases fasting duration mid-week, starting with standard 16:8 protocol on Monday and Thursday, extending to 18:6 on Wednesday, and attempting 20:4 on Friday. The weekend maintains moderate fasting to provide recovery time and sustain momentum.

**Meal Timing and Composition Guidelines**:

**Breakfast/First Meal**: Start with protein-rich, nutrient-dense foods to initiate metabolism and provide sustained energy. Options include: eggs, Greek yogurt, protein smoothies, lean protein with vegetables, or oatmeal with nuts. Focus on high-quality proteins (20-30g) to maintain muscle mass and blood sugar stability.

**Lunch/Second Meal**: Balance macronutrients with 40% protein, 30% healthy fats, and 30% complex carbohydrates. Include vegetables for fiber and micronutrients. Portions should be satisfying but not excessive, focusing on nutrient density over caloric quantity.

**Dinner/Third Meal**: Keep portion sizes moderate and finish eating by the end of the eating window. Prioritize easily digestible proteins (fish, poultry, legumes) and vegetables, avoiding heavy carbohydrates late in the day to support sleep quality.

**Hydration Protocol**:
Maintain consistent water intake throughout the fasting window: 8-10 glasses daily. During fasting periods, drink water, herbal tea, or black coffee without sugar or cream. Avoid calorie-containing beverages including juices, sports drinks, and smoothies. Electrolyte supplementation may be beneficial during longer fasting periods, particularly potassium and magnesium.

**Support Systems**:

**Environmental Cues**: Place water bottles prominently, set meal timing reminders, and prepare fasting-friendly snacks (nuts, seeds, vegetables) for emergency hunger management. Remove tempting foods from easy access.

**Social Support**: Inform household members about fasting schedule to avoid mealtime pressure and encourage mutual adherence. Share progress with accountability partners or online communities.

**Technical Support**: Use fitness tracking apps to log fasting periods, meals, and energy levels. Set up calendar reminders for eating windows and prepare meal plans in advance to reduce decision fatigue.

**Progress Tracking**:
Daily tracking includes: fasting duration (hours), meals consumed (quantity/quality), water intake (glasses), energy levels (1-10 scale), physical symptoms (hunger, headache, dizziness), and weight measurement. Weekly review analyzes patterns, identifies challenges, and adjusts approach as needed.

**Adaptation Strategies**:
If experiencing significant discomfort or compromised performance, reduce fasting duration: extend eating windows to 9-10 hours or increase meal frequency to 2-3 smaller meals within the window. Temporary breaks (eating window extension to 10-12 hours) are acceptable for special occasions or increased physical demands.

**Safety Protocols**:
Monitor for signs of disordered eating, excessive fatigue, or nutritional deficiencies. Consult healthcare provider before beginning IF, especially if pregnant, breastfeeding, or managing medical conditions. Stop IF immediately if experiencing severe symptoms including fainting, persistent dizziness, or prolonged lethargy.

## Decisions and Assumptions

**Decision 1: Progressive Fasting Duration**
The schedule gradually increases fasting duration from 16:8 to 20:4 over the week, rather than starting with the most demanding protocol immediately. This approach reduces initial adaptation stress and minimizes risk of premature abandonment. Alternative approaches (constant 16:8 or daily 20:4) were considered but rejected due to higher adaptation difficulty and potential for burnout.

**Decision 2: Flexible Eating Windows**
Eating windows shift daily (12:00 PM to 8:00 PM vs. 1:00 PM to 9:00 PM) to accommodate varying daily schedules and social commitments. This flexibility improves adherence while maintaining overall fasting duration. Fixed windows were rejected as they create scheduling conflicts.

**Decision 3: Nutrient Priority Over Caloric Restriction**
The protocol emphasizes nutrient density and adequate protein intake rather than calorie limitation. Meals should provide sufficient calories for energy needs, with focus on quality foods. Calorie restriction was avoided to prevent metabolic slowdown and nutrient deficiencies.

**Decision 4: Hydration-First Approach**
Water intake is emphasized over calorie consumption during fasting periods. Calorie-free beverages are encouraged to maintain hydration without breaking the fast. This supports metabolic function and reduces hunger intensity.

**Decision 5: Weekly Review and Adjustment**
The system incorporates regular review periods (daily tracking, weekly assessment) to evaluate adherence and adjust protocol as needed. This adaptive approach ensures sustainability and addresses individual variations in response to intermittent fasting.
