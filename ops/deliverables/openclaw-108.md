## Prioritization Rules

### Family Outing Priority Framework

**Rule 1: Regular Consistency Over Intensity**
- 2-3 quality outings per month create better habits than occasional intensive trips
- Consistency builds anticipation and reduces planning stress
- Regular outings become part of family rhythm and identity

**Rule 2: Balance All Ages and Interests**
- Activities must be enjoyable for everyone from youngest to oldest
- Include variety to prevent burnout
- Allow individual preferences within structured group activities

**Rule 3: Plan-Execution Balance**
- Some planning is essential but over-planning reduces flexibility
- Build buffer time and alternatives into plans
- Maintain "plan A, B, and C" mentality

**Rule 4: Start Small and Grow**
- Begin with closer, lower-effort outings if not currently going out
- Gradually increase complexity and distance
- Build experience and confidence in planning and executing

**Rule 5: Quality > Quantity**
- One excellent outing is better than three mediocre ones
- Focus on creating meaningful experiences rather than checking boxes
- Prioritize connection and quality time over activity variety

**Rule 6: Seasonal Adaptation**
- Adjust outing types based on season and weather
- Plan indoor alternatives for inclement weather
- Embrace seasonal-specific activities

## Review Cadence

### Weekly Review (End of Each Week)

**Time**: Sunday Evening, 15 minutes
**Purpose**: Ensure next week's outing is properly planned, make adjustments based on previous outing

**Review Questions**:
1. Is the next week's outing planned with adequate time?
2. Are all family members aware of the outing details?
3. What went well with recent outings to repeat?
4. What needs improvement in planning and execution?

**Review Actions**:
- Confirm next week's outing plans
- Check planning timeline (7 days before review)
- Address any issues from recent outings
- Make adjustments for upcoming outing

### Monthly Review (End of Each Month)

**Time**: Last Sunday Evening, 30 minutes
**Purpose**: Evaluate overall outing pattern, family satisfaction, and plan improvements

**Review Questions**:
1. Are we going out frequently enough?
2. Are family members enjoying the outings?
3. Is the planning process sustainable?
4. What activities and locations work best?
5. What improvements can be made?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete monthly review template
- Analyze outing patterns and satisfaction
- Update preferences and interests
- Plan improvements for next month
- Adjust frequency or planning approach if needed

### Quarterly Review (End of Each Quarter)

**Time**: Last Sunday Evening, 45 minutes
**Purpose**: Comprehensive assessment of family outing system and long-term planning

**Review Questions**:
1. Are family outings a consistent part of our routine?
2. What patterns have emerged in preferences and satisfaction?
3. Is the current planning system sustainable long-term?
4. What new activities and locations should we explore?
5. Are we achieving our goals for quality family time?

**Review Actions**:
- Complete quarterly review template
- Analyze long-term trends and patterns
- Set quarterly goals for family outings
- Refine system for ongoing effectiveness
- Plan special seasonal activities
