Learning experiences that actually work

I design learning solutions that engage people and deliver measurable outcomes. Not just content that gets completed, but experiences that change behavior and build capability.

How I Think About Learning

Every project starts with these principles. They shape every decision, from strategy to the smallest interaction detail.

Outcomes First, Always

Before we discuss formats or tools, we get clear on what success looks like. What will learners be able to do differently? How will we know it worked? Everything flows from there.

Transfer is the Goal

Completion rates are easy to measure but often meaningless. I focus on designing for transfer, ensuring learning actually applies in the real world, not just during the course.

Science-Backed Creativity

Creative solutions grounded in cognitive science. Spaced practice, retrieval, worked examples, cognitive load management, all woven into experiences that feel fresh and engaging.

Strategic Gamification

Gamification isn't points and badges. It's understanding motivation and designing for intrinsic engagement. When done right, it transforms how people approach learning.

The Process

Every engagement is different, but here's how I typically approach a project. Flexible when needed, structured where it matters.

01

Understand the Real Problem

Most learning challenges aren't what they first appear to be. I dig into the actual performance gap, the context, the constraints, and what's been tried before. This shapes everything that follows.

02

Define Success Clearly

What does success look like, concretely? We establish observable outcomes, how we'll measure them, and what "good enough" means. This prevents scope creep and keeps the solution focused.

03

Design the Experience

This is where creativity meets science. I develop the learning architecture, engagement mechanics, and key interactions, prototyping and testing concepts before full development.

04

Build with Precision

Detailed design that brings the concept to life. Every interaction, every piece of feedback, every visual decision is intentional, driving toward the defined outcomes.

05

Measure and Iterate

Data-informed refinement. What's working? What's not? Learning analytics reveal the story, and we optimize based on evidence, not assumptions.

The Rare Combination

Most learning designers fall into one of two camps. Either they're creative but light on learning science, producing experiences that look great but don't drive results. Or they're rigorous but dull, technically sound but nobody wants to engage with what they build.

I bring both. A background in advertising taught me how to capture attention and create emotional connection. Years in learning science taught me how to make that engagement translate into actual capability building. The result is work that people want to experience, and that delivers measurable outcomes.
